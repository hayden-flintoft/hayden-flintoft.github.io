Introducing Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health.

2024-07-06

Tā Mason Durie's Te Whare Tapa Whā model is a holistic approach to health and wellbeing rooted in Māori values, emphasizing the balance between four cornerstones: Taha Tinana (physical health), Taha Hinengaro (mental health), Taha Whānau (family health), and Taha Wairua (spiritual health). Here's my wellbeing plan based on this model:

1. Taha tinana (Physical Health)

Exercise: Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate physical activity most days of the week.

Nutrition: Focus on a balanced diet with traditional and nutritious foods.

Rest: Ensure 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night with a consistent bedtime routine.

2. Taha Wairua (Cultural/Spiritual Health)

Connection to Cultural Heritage: Engage in activities that connect with my cultural heritage, such as visiting landmarks, participating in local cultural events, and exploring my family's history.

Personal Development: Pursue personal interests and activities that enrich my sense of identity and personal fulfilment, such as gardening, hiking, or creative arts.

Reflection and Purpose: Reflect on my life's purpose and ensure my actions align with my values.

3. Taha whānau (Family Health)

Quality Time: Dedicate time for family activities that enhance togetherness.

Communication: Maintain open and honest communication with my family.

Support Systems: Build and maintain a supportive network of family and friends.

4. Taha Hinengaro (Mental Health)

Mindfulness and Meditation: Practice daily to improve focus and reduce stress.

Learning and Growth: Continue advancing in HTML, CSS, Javascript, and REACT.

Check-ins: Regularly Check-in with Family, Homegroup Members, Course Facilitators and Internally to help limit stress, ADHD, and anxiety within myself and those around me.

5. Whenua (Connection to The Land)

Take time to experience nature in my everyday environment.

Appreciate the rustling of leaves, the patterns of flowers (once they bloom), or the sunset from my lounge window.
